Where to bet in Alberta's regulated market

As of July 13, 2026, Alberta's open iGaming market means real, licensed sportsbooks are legally available in-province. Here's a practical rundown for NHL bettors — specifically, which books carry the market types our signals and prop models are built to analyze.

Only bet with operators licensed in your province, and only bet what you can afford to lose.

What matters for our signals

Our game signals (Signal 1, Signal 1 Partial, Signal 2) are moneyline plays — every licensed book carries those. Where books genuinely differ is player prop depth: our prop model compares its own probability estimates against real market prices on shots on goal, points, assists, and anytime goal scorer markets. The more of those markets a book posts (and the earlier in the day they post them), the more of our board you can actually act on.

The other practical difference is price. The same prop is often listed at different odds across books — and our model's edge calculations are only as good as the price you actually get. Having accounts at two or three books and taking the best available number is the single easiest ROI improvement most bettors never make.
